Case Study: Measuring the ROI of Workforce Support Systems
The Problem

Organizations invest in employee support programs but often struggle to quantify their impact on performance and retention. Leadership needed a data-driven way to determine whether structured support models improved operational outcomes.

Approach

I designed a multi-part analytics study using synthetic but realistic workforce data to model:

Productivity differences between supported and unsupported employees

Psychological safety as a predictor of retention

Long-term performance trends

Financial ROI of support interventions

Methods included:

ANOVA to test productivity differences

Logistic regression for retention modeling

Time-series trend analysis

ROI modeling tied to operational value metrics

Key Insights

Structured support increased productivity by ~23%

Psychological safety significantly improved retention likelihood

Productivity gains compounded over time

Support programs showed a ~500% estimated ROI

Impact

This analysis reframes employee enablement as a business performance strategy, not just an HR initiative — aligning workforce wellbeing with operational and financial outcomes.

Tools Used

Python, Pandas, Statsmodels, Scikit-Learn, Seaborn, Matplotlib
